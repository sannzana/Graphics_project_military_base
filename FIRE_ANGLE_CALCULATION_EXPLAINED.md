## KEY INSIGHT: WHY THIS FORMULA?

```
The formula (7 - layerIndex) / 6.0 creates:

• Layers with LOWER indices (1, 2, 3) ? HIGHER speed factors
• Layers with HIGHER indices (4, 5, 6) ? LOWER speed factors

Why?
  Layer 1 (base) needs to rotate SLOWLY to seem stable
  Layer 6 (tip) needs to rotate FAST to seem like flickering

Result:
  Non-linear motion creates realistic fire effect
  Different layers move at different rates
  Creates complex, non-repetitive animation
  Looks like real flames dancing
```

**"The rotation angle for each fire layer is decided by a three-step formula:**

**Step 1: Calculate speed factor based on layer index**
```
speedFactor = (7 - layerIndex) / 6.0
```

**Step 2: Multiply by base speed to get layer-specific rotation velocity**
```
layerSpeed = 180 degrees/second × speedFactor
```

**Step 3: Multiply by elapsed time to accumulate the angle**
```
rotationAngle = layerSpeed × t
```

**Where t is the elapsed time from glfwGetTime(). This ensures different layers rotate at different speeds—Layer 1 (base) rotates at 180°/second while Layer 6 (top) rotates at only 30°/second.
